# Harmonics and octaves

Octaves are just a special case of harmonics, where they are the power of 2 (2,4,8,16,...) multiples of the fundamental frequency.

Harmonics refer to all the multiples (2,3,4,5,...) of the fundamental.

ex. A = 110 Hz

1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8

110, 220, 330, 440 , 550, 660, 770, 880, ...

A2, A3, E3, A4, C#4, E4, G4, A5, ....

The bold number are the octaves of A=110Hz.

# Frequencies in a scale

There are 12 notes in a western musical scale. (C, C#, D, D#, E, F...)

As seen from above, an octave is referred to the same note, but double the frequency of the given note.

freq_{octave} = 2 \cdot freq_{original} \\

Between octaves, there are 12 notes and we want to have equal distance between these notes.

This is why between each adjacent note in a scale, the frequency is 21/122^{1/12}2​1/12​​ times the previous one (2(1/12)∗12=22^{(1/12)*12} = 22​(1/12)∗12​​=2).
